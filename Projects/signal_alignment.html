Elecropherogram Alignment

While there is no cure to Parkinson’s disease, the onset of symptoms could potentially be delayed with more accurate, early diagnosis.

This study examined optimizing a tool to be utilized when analyzing data to determine relationships between the amount of certain molecules in the plasma of Parkinson's patients, thus discovering the identification of molecular fingerprints unique to Parkinson's disease pathology.

The study examines two approaches to capillary zone electrophoresis alignment: the linear and chained approaches. Both algorithms begin by calculating the total DTW distance for each signal against every other signal, and storing the results in a matrix. Then the algorithms sum the combined distances for each signal, and finding the signal with the minimum sum. Then the algorithms take different approaches in which signals are aligned next:

Linear Approach

Aligns every other signal with the signal with the lowest total DTW distance

Chained Approach

Aligns signals with the signal that has the lowest total Euclidean distance from itself

After aligning the signals using DTW, both algorithms align the widths of every peak using interpolation

Results

While this analysis does not take into account over-fitting of the signals from DTW alignment, the chained alignment results in the greatest improvement.
This approach could be implemented in wider studies examining the amount of molecules present in the blood stream of Parkinson’s patients.
